Auditory Perception Experiment - Judgements of Speech Sounds

This research examines how we process sounds. You will be asked to listen to different types of sounds and we will ask you to respond to these sounds via button presses.

After completing a basic demographic questionnaire, you will be asked to complete an initial screening task. If you perform sufficiently on the screening portion, you will have the opportunity to proceed to the main experiment.

The screening task is brief, and will take no more than 5 minutes to complete. You will be compensated $ 0.25 for completing this task. The main experiment will take about 30 minutes to complete, and you will receive a bonus payment of $ 2.50 for successfully completing all trials.

We do not ask for your name or any other information that might identify you. Your participation in this research study is voluntary. You may withdraw at any time, but you must proceed to the final screen of the study in order to submit your results and receive compensation.

In accordance with Mechanical Turk policies, we may reject your work if the HIT was not completed correctly or the instructions were not followed.

If you have any questions about the research please contact us at sound-lab@duke.edu. If you have questions about your rights as a research subject, contact the Duke University research review committee at campusirb@duke.edu or at 919-684-3030. Speech Quilts

This experiment uses a set of auditory stimuli that we call "Speech Quilts". We call these Speech Quilts because they are composed of shorter segments of speech, which have been scrambled and "stitched" together. The language and how long the segments are that make up a speech quilt will vary across trials.

Click on the buttons below to get a sense of what the speech quilts sound like

The time durations listed on the buttons reflect the length of the speech segments which make up the corresponding speech quilt.

You should be able to hear that the segment length increases as you progress down the list.

Press 'Continue' to proceed.

Practice Trials

In each trial, you will listen to two 2-second long "Speech Quilts". These will be just like the examples you heard on the previous page. The language and how long the segments are that make up a speech quilt will vary across trials.

Your task is to judge which sound clip is composed of shorter segments. You will do this by selecting either Example 1 or Example 2 by pressing on the corresponding button.

You will receive feedback after each trial about whether your response was correct or incorrect. There will be a total of 8 practice trials. The first four should be quite easy, while the following four will be more difficult.

If you are ever unsure, just take your best guess and keep going.

Let's give it a try!

Start Practice

You are about to begin the main experiment.

The following trials will be similar to the practice trials you've just completed. The first trial will start as soon as you hit the start button below.

In each trial, you will be presented with two speech quilts, and your job will be to determine which one was made up of shorter segments. You will make your selection by pressing on the corresponding example button just as you did in the practice section. The experiment will then proceed on to the next trial. You will not receive feedback during this section.

There will be a progress bar at the top of the screen so you can monitor your how long you have to go.

Please know that we hope to use this data to further our understanding of how the human brain processes and understands speech, so it is imperative that you stay focused and do your best with the task.

Thanks, and good luck!
